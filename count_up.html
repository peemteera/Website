Count Up

CountUp is an O2O platform that creates and delivers content for the Silver Generation (50 years +) to promote a healthy, active lifestyle.

This was a 3-month project where I redesigned the company’s mobile responsive web in order to increase the overall reading statistics.

The Problem
Users who finish reading one story are often motivated to read another. And yet, users are having difficulties in finding related stories, understanding the topic categories, or simply navigating to our homepage (most users enter our site through social media platforms and waste their time backtracking to the original source to find more stories).

Our Goal
To help our readers find related stories in a quicker and more intuitive method, and introduce a simple user flow where readers can navigate easily between the different pages.

ROLE
User Research, Information Architecture, Mobile User Experience Design, Interaction Design, Prototyping

TIMELINE
Sept 2019 - Dec 2019

PLATFORM
Responsive Web (iOS)

Step 1: User Research

Research Methods
To redesign the mobile site, we had to address our users’ reading habits and also pain points about the current navigation features. We decided to select two different research methodologies to inspect each of these elements.

For the purpose of our two research interests, we believed that a user survey will help us gather data on general reading habits and objectives while a user interview will give us a deeper understanding of user interaction and thinking process.

User Interviews
To understand the thought process behind these statistics, we conducted 5 user interviews/testing to probe some of these findings. We asked the same questions as the survey, but also provided a task for users to complete in order to observe their user journey:

  1. Can you find a story from the ‘Food’ category?
  2. Can you take me to the homepage and describe what you see?
  3. If you have to find stories related to the one you are reading now, how would you go about it?

Step 3: Information Architecture

Site Map
Based on the pain points, we wanted to restructure the hierarchy and navigation structure of the website. As of now, most of our navigation features are embedded on the homepage. But if users never reach the homepage anyway, then let’s move them onto the story page itself!

We created a new flow where users can directly transition from one story to the next, either by categories or related topics.
